Koreatown is under crisis

  • The Issue
  • The Solution
  • External Resources

Koreatown is a neighborhood located a little west of Downtown Los Angeles. This neighborhood has been historically known to reside low-income and immigrant families. However, there has been an increase in gentrification. Many landlords have sold their properties to developers. These developers evict tenants to remodel buildings or construct newer buildings. This has led to the displacement of those who cannot afford these high rents. A place that was once known to be a safe place for low-income families has turned into an increasingly gentrified and expensive neighborhood. It is extremely important to spread awareness due to the increase in rents. Many people end up looking for other places to live and they are no longer locals of the area in which they once resided.

The Solution

  • Tenant Protections: City and States should increase legal protection for tenants. These can include things like protection from unfair evictions and ensuring tenant rights. Tenants should also be provided with legal aid and support.
  • Affordable Housing Policies: States should implement and enforce rent control policies that prevent intense rent increases. Affordable housing policies should also include affordable housing programs that provide affordable housing to low-income families. These could come in the form of requiring developers to include a certain percentage of affordable units in new buildings.
  • Raise Community Voices: Engage members of the community in the planning and decision-making process to ensure that newer projects meet their needs.
The Fight Against Grentrification

Optimism for the future

Gentrification in Koreatown will continue to increase. However, as previously mentioned, residents must unite and advocate for their community. Unity to save the culture of Koreatown is needed. Only then will voices be heard and more policies implemented. Affordable housing initiatives and inclusionary zoning are achievable. There can be gradual change, but only if residents stand up for the needs of their community.
